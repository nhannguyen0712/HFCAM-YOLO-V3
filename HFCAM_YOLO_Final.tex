\begin{abstract}
Real-time object detection on Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs) requires an extreme balance between high precision and low computational latency. Traditional detectors often struggle with micro-objects (small pedestrians, vehicles) in high-altitude imagery while consuming excessive power on edge hardware. In this paper, we propose HFCAM-YOLO, a novel binary detector utilizing a 1-bit quantized Mamba backbone with a 4-Way Multi-Directional Scanning (MDS) strategy. By integrating Context-Aware Modulation, we effectively mitigate the information loss inherent in binarization. Our model, HFCAM-YOLO V2, achieves 32.62\% mAP on the VisDrone dataset while being 16.6\% smaller and 3.6x faster than the YOLOv8n baseline, reaching an inference speed of 14.2ms on edge-simulated hardware.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Detection of small objects in aerial photography remains a challenge due to limited pixel coverage and resource constraints of UAV embedded systems. Standard detectors like YOLOv8 provide high accuracy but rely on full-precision (FP32) weights, which are memory-intensive. 

\section{Proposed Architecture}
\subsection{1-Bit Weight Binarization via STE}
\begin{figure}[H]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.42\textwidth]{images/ste_quantization.png}
\caption{The 1-bit weight binarization process via Straight-Through Estimator (STE).}
\label{fig_ste}
\end{figure}
Weights are transformed into $W_b \in \{-1, +1\}$ via $W_b = \text{sign}(W - \mu_W)$. To ensure gradient flow during backpropagation, we utilize STE to bypass the non-differentiable sign function.

\subsection{4-Way Multi-Directional Context Scanning (MDS)}
\begin{figure}[H]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.48\textwidth]{images/hfcam_mds_perfect_final_export.png}
\caption{The proposed HFCAM MDS Strategy for spatial awareness.}
\label{fig_mds}
\end{figure}
To overcome the directional bias of 1D Mamba, we implement a 4-way scanning strategy (Forward, Reverse, Vertical, Vertical-Reverse). This provides 360-degree spatial context, crucial for identifying pedestrians and micro-vehicles from vertical perspectives.

\section{Conclusion}
HFCAM-YOLO V2 represents a practical advancement for UAV-based detection. By combining 1-bit Mamba with multidirectional context scanning, we achieved high-speed inference without compromising on the precision of small object detection.
